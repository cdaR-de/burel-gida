# ISO 22000 Food Safety Management System Guide

ISO 22000 is an international standard that specifies requirements for a food safety management system (FSMS) to demonstrate an organization's ability to control food safety hazards and ensure food is safe for consumption.

## Understanding ISO 22000

### What is ISO 22000?

ISO 22000:2018 is the latest version of the international food safety management standard. It combines and supplements the core elements of ISO 9001 and HACCP to provide a comprehensive framework for managing food safety.

### Who Should Implement ISO 22000?

ISO 22000 is applicable to all organizations in the food chain, including:
- Primary producers (farms, fisheries)
- Food manufacturers and processors
- Food packaging manufacturers
- Transportation and storage operators
- Retail and food service operations
- Equipment manufacturers
- Cleaning and sanitation service providers
- Ingredient and additive suppliers

### Benefits of ISO 22000 Certification

**Business Benefits**:
- Enhanced market access and competitiveness
- Improved customer confidence and satisfaction
- Reduced liability and insurance costs
- Streamlined operations and reduced waste
- Better supplier relationships
- Facilitated international trade

**Operational Benefits**:
- Systematic approach to food safety
- Integration with other management systems
- Improved communication throughout food chain
- Continuous improvement culture
- Better resource management
- Enhanced traceability

**Compliance Benefits**:
- Demonstrates due diligence
- Meets regulatory requirements
- Provides defense in legal situations
- Facilitates audits and inspections

## Structure of ISO 22000:2018

ISO 22000 follows the High-Level Structure (HLS) common to all ISO management system standards, making integration easier.

### Clause Structure

**Clause 1-3**: Scope, Normative References, Terms and Definitions
**Clause 4**: Context of the Organization
**Clause 5**: Leadership
**Clause 6**: Planning
**Clause 7**: Support
**Clause 8**: Operation
**Clause 9**: Performance Evaluation
**Clause 10**: Improvement

## Key Requirements Explained

### Clause 4: Context of the Organization

#### 4.1 Understanding the Organization and Its Context

Identify internal and external issues that affect your FSMS:

**External Issues**:
- Regulatory environment
- Market conditions
- Technological developments
- Competitive landscape
- Cultural and social factors
- Economic conditions

**Internal Issues**:
- Organizational culture
- Resources and capabilities
- Infrastructure
- Knowledge and competencies
- Processes and systems

**Tools for Analysis**:
- SWOT analysis
- PESTLE analysis
- Stakeholder mapping
- Risk assessment

#### 4.2 Understanding the Needs and Expectations of Interested Parties

Identify stakeholders and their requirements:

**Key Stakeholders**:
- Customers and consumers
- Regulatory authorities
- Suppliers and contractors
- Employees
- Shareholders/owners
- Industry associations
- Local communities

**Requirements to Consider**:
- Food safety requirements
- Legal and regulatory requirements
- Customer specifications
- Industry standards
- Contractual obligations

#### 4.3 Determining the Scope of the FSMS

Define boundaries and applicability:
- Products and services covered
- Processes included
- Sites and locations
- Exclusions (with justification)

#### 4.4 Food Safety Management System

Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve the FSMS:
- Determine processes and interactions
- Establish process criteria
- Allocate resources
- Monitor and measure processes
- Implement improvements

### Clause 5: Leadership

#### 5.1 Leadership and Commitment

Top management must demonstrate leadership by:
- Taking accountability for FSMS effectiveness
- Ensuring food safety policy and objectives align with strategy
- Integrating FSMS into business processes
- Promoting process approach and risk-based thinking
- Ensuring resources are available
- Communicating importance of effective FSMS
- Ensuring FSMS achieves intended outcomes
- Supporting other management roles
- Promoting continuous improvement
- Supporting food safety culture

#### 5.2 Food Safety Policy

Develop a food safety policy that:
- Is appropriate to the organization
- Provides framework for objectives
- Includes commitment to meet requirements
- Includes commitment to continuous improvement
- Is documented, communicated, and understood
- Is available to interested parties

**Example Policy Statement**:
"[Company Name] is committed to producing safe, high-quality food products. We will:
- Comply with all applicable food safety regulations
- Implement and maintain an effective FSMS based on ISO 22000
- Continuously improve our food safety performance
- Communicate openly with customers and stakeholders
- Provide necessary resources and training
- Foster a culture of food safety throughout our organization"

#### 5.3 Organizational Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities

Define and communicate:
- FSMS responsibilities and authorities
- Food safety team leader role
- Reporting relationships
- Authority to take corrective actions
- Communication channels

**Key Roles**:
- **Top Management**: Overall accountability
- **Food Safety Team Leader**: Manages FSMS, reports to top management
- **Food Safety Team**: Develops and maintains FSMS
- **Process Owners**: Responsible for specific processes
- **All Personnel**: Follow food safety procedures

### Clause 6: Planning

#### 6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities

Implement risk-based thinking:
- Identify risks and opportunities
- Plan actions to address them
- Integrate actions into FSMS processes
- Evaluate effectiveness of actions

**Risk Assessment Process**:
1. Identify hazards and risks
2. Analyze likelihood and severity
3. Evaluate risk significance
4. Determine control measures
5. Monitor and review

#### 6.2 Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them

Establish food safety objectives that are:
- Consistent with food safety policy
- Measurable
- Monitored
- Communicated
- Updated as appropriate

**SMART Objectives**:
- **S**pecific: Clearly defined
- **M**easurable: Quantifiable
- **A**chievable: Realistic
- **R**elevant: Aligned with policy
- **T**ime-bound: Deadline specified

**Example Objectives**:
- Reduce customer complaints by 25% within 12 months
- Achieve zero critical non-conformances in external audits
- Complete 100% of planned FSMS training by Q2
- Reduce product waste by 15% through improved controls

#### 6.3 Planning of Changes

Manage changes systematically:
- Identify need for change
- Assess potential consequences
- Ensure integrity of FSMS maintained
- Allocate resources
- Assign responsibilities
- Communicate changes

### Clause 7: Support

#### 7.1 Resources

Provide resources for FSMS:
- People
- Infrastructure
- Work environment
- External resources

**Infrastructure Requirements**:
- Buildings and utilities
- Process equipment
- Transportation
- Information and communication technology

**Work Environment**:
- Physical factors (temperature, humidity, lighting)
- Social factors (non-discriminatory, calm)
- Psychological factors (stress-reducing)

#### 7.2 Competence

Ensure personnel competence:
- Determine required competencies
- Provide training or take other actions
- Evaluate effectiveness of actions
- Retain documented information

**Competency Areas**:
- Food safety principles
- HACCP principles
- Relevant regulations
- Process-specific skills
- Equipment operation
- Hygiene practices
- Emergency procedures

#### 7.3 Awareness

Ensure personnel are aware of:
- Food safety policy
- Their contribution to FSMS effectiveness
- Implications of not conforming to FSMS requirements
- Relevant food safety hazards and control measures

#### 7.4 Communication

Establish internal and external communication:

**Internal Communication**:
- Between different levels
- Between different functions
- Changes to FSMS
- Food safety hazards and controls

**External Communication**:
- With suppliers and contractors
- With customers
- With regulatory authorities
- With other interested parties

**Communication Methods**:
- Meetings and briefings
- Newsletters and bulletins
- Intranet and email
- Training sessions
- Notice boards
- Reports and documentation

#### 7.5 Documented Information

Control documented information:
- Create and update
- Control distribution
- Protect from unauthorized changes
- Ensure availability and suitability
- Control changes
- Retain and dispose appropriately

**Required Documentation**:
- FSMS scope
- Food safety policy
- Risk and opportunity assessment
- Food safety objectives
- Hazard analysis
- Operational PRPs
- HACCP plan
- Control measures
- Monitoring procedures
- Verification procedures
- Traceability system
- Emergency procedures

### Clause 8: Operation

#### 8.1 Operational Planning and Control

Plan, implement, and control processes:
- Establish process criteria
- Implement process controls
- Control planned changes
- Control outsourced processes

#### 8.2 Prerequisite Programs (PRPs)

Establish, implement, and maintain PRPs:

**Essential PRPs**:
- Infrastructure and maintenance
- Cleaning and sanitation
- Pest control
- Personnel hygiene
- Supplier management
- Storage and transportation
- Cross-contamination prevention
- Allergen management
- Water, air, and energy management
- Waste management
- Product recall procedures

#### 8.3 Traceability System

Establish system to identify products:
- From incoming materials
- Through all stages of production
- To delivery of finished products
- Enable product withdrawal/recall
- Maintain records for defined period

**Traceability Requirements**:
- Unique identification for batches
- Link to raw materials and ingredients
- Link to processing records
- Link to distribution records
- Ability to trace one step forward and one step back

#### 8.4 Emergency Preparedness and Response

Plan for potential emergencies:
- Identify potential emergency situations
- Establish emergency procedures
- Test emergency procedures
- Review and update procedures
- Communicate with interested parties

**Emergency Scenarios**:
- Product contamination
- Equipment failure
- Utility disruptions
- Natural disasters
- Security threats
- Pandemic situations

#### 8.5 Hazard Control

##### 8.5.1 Preliminary Steps to Enable Hazard Analysis

**8.5.1.1 General**: Gather information needed for hazard analysis

**8.5.1.2 Characteristics of Raw Materials, Ingredients, and Product-Contact Materials**:
- Biological, chemical, physical characteristics
- Composition and formulation
- Origin and source
- Production method
- Packaging and delivery
- Storage conditions and shelf life
- Preparation and handling before use

**8.5.1.3 Characteristics of End Products**:
- Product name and description
- Composition
- Relevant food safety characteristics
- Intended shelf life and storage conditions
- Packaging
- Labeling and handling instructions
- Distribution methods

**8.5.1.4 Intended Use**:
- Expected use by customer/consumer
- Target consumer groups
- Vulnerable populations

**8.5.1.5 Flow Diagrams and Process Descriptions**:
- Detailed flow diagrams for all products
- Description of processes and equipment
- Verification of flow diagrams on-site

##### 8.5.2 Hazard Analysis

**8.5.2.1 Identify Hazards and Determine Acceptable Levels**:
- List all potential hazards
- Identify where hazards may occur
- Determine acceptable levels

**8.5.2.2 Hazard Assessment**:
- Assess severity and likelihood
- Determine if hazard requires control

**8.5.2.3 Selection and Categorization of Control Measures**:
- Select appropriate control measures
- Categorize as PRP, operational PRP, or CCP

##### 8.5.3 Validation of Control Measures

Validate that control measures are capable of achieving intended control

##### 8.5.4 HACCP Plan

Establish HACCP plan including:
- Critical control points (CCPs)
- Critical limits for each CCP
- Monitoring systems
- Corrective actions
- Verification procedures

#### 8.6 Updating Information Specifying PRPs and HACCP Plan

Update when necessary based on:
- Changes to products, processes, or equipment
- New hazard information
- Verification results
- Emerging hazards

#### 8.7 Control of Monitoring and Measuring

Ensure monitoring and measuring equipment is:
- Suitable for purpose
- Calibrated or verified
- Identified and protected
- Records maintained

#### 8.8 Verification Related to PRPs and HACCP Plan

Verify that:
- PRPs are implemented
- Hazard control plan is implemented
- Hazard levels are within acceptable limits
- Other procedures are implemented

#### 8.9 Control of Product and Process Nonconformities

##### 8.9.1 Nonconformities Related to Critical Limits

When critical limits are exceeded:
- Identify and control affected product
- Evaluate for release
- Take corrective actions
- Prevent recurrence

##### 8.9.2 Nonconformities Related to Monitoring Results

Handle deviations from operational PRPs

##### 8.9.3 Nonconformities Detected by Verification

Address nonconformities found during verification

##### 8.9.4 Corrections and Corrective Actions

Implement corrections (immediate actions) and corrective actions (prevent recurrence)

##### 8.9.5 Handling of Potentially Unsafe Products

**8.9.5.1 Evaluation for Release**: Assess if product can be released

**8.9.5.2 Product Disposition**: Determine appropriate action:
- Release for intended use
- Release for alternative use
- Rework
- Destruction

**8.9.5.3 Product Withdrawal and Recall**:
- Establish withdrawal/recall procedures
- Notify interested parties
- Handle withdrawn/recalled products
- Sequence of actions for recall

### Clause 9: Performance Evaluation

#### 9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation

Determine:
- What needs monitoring and measurement
- Methods for monitoring and measurement
- When to perform monitoring and measurement
- When to analyze and evaluate results

**Key Performance Indicators**:
- CCP monitoring results
- Verification results
- Customer feedback
- Supplier performance
- Training completion rates
- Audit findings
- Nonconformity trends

#### 9.2 Internal Audit

Conduct internal audits to verify FSMS:
- Conforms to requirements
- Is effectively implemented and maintained

**Audit Program**:
- Plan audit schedule
- Define audit criteria and scope
- Select competent auditors
- Ensure objectivity and impartiality
- Report audit results
- Take corrective actions
- Retain documented information

#### 9.3 Management Review

Top management reviews FSMS at planned intervals:

**Review Inputs**:
- Status of previous management review actions
- Changes in internal and external issues
- Information on FSMS performance
- Adequacy of resources
- Effectiveness of actions to address risks
- Opportunities for improvement

**Review Outputs**:
- Opportunities for improvement
- Need for changes to FSMS
- Resource needs
- Actions required

### Clause 10: Improvement

#### 10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action

When nonconformity occurs:
- React and take action to control and correct
- Evaluate need for action to eliminate causes
- Implement corrective actions
- Review effectiveness of corrective actions
- Update FSMS if necessary
- Retain documented information

#### 10.2 Continual Improvement

Continually improve:
- Suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of FSMS
- Update hazard analysis
- Update PRPs and HACCP plan
- Reevaluate and update FSMS

#### 10.3 Update of the FSMS

Ensure FSMS is updated based on:
- Communication from external and internal sources
- Information about food safety hazards
- Verification results
- Management review outputs

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Preparation (Months 1-2)
- Gain management commitment
- Appoint food safety team leader
- Assemble food safety team
- Provide ISO 22000 training
- Conduct gap analysis

### Phase 2: Documentation (Months 3-4)
- Define FSMS scope
- Develop food safety policy
- Document processes
- Create required procedures
- Establish PRPs

### Phase 3: Hazard Analysis and HACCP (Months 5-6)
- Conduct hazard analysis
- Determine control measures
- Establish HACCP plan
- Validate control measures
- Develop monitoring procedures

### Phase 4: Implementation (Months 7-9)
- Implement PRPs
- Implement HACCP plan
- Train all personnel
- Begin monitoring and recording
- Establish communication processes

### Phase 5: Verification (Months 10-11)
- Conduct internal audits
- Perform verification activities
- Review and analyze data
- Implement improvements
- Conduct management review

### Phase 6: Certification (Month 12)
- Select certification body
- Stage 1 audit (documentation review)
- Address any findings
- Stage 2 audit (implementation review)
- Achieve certification

## Maintaining Certification

### Surveillance Audits
- Typically conducted annually
- Verify continued compliance
- Review changes and improvements
- Address any nonconformities

### Recertification
- Every 3 years
- Comprehensive audit
- Demonstrates continued effectiveness
- Opportunity for improvement

## Integration with Other Standards

### ISO 22000 and ISO 9001
- Compatible structures (HLS)
- Can be integrated into single management system
- Shared processes and documentation
- Combined audits possible

### ISO 22000 and FSSC 22000
- FSSC 22000 builds on ISO 22000
- Adds additional requirements
- Recognized by GFSI
- Required by many retailers

- Continuous improvement mindset

The investment in ISO 22000 certification pays dividends through improved food safety, operational efficiency, and market access.
